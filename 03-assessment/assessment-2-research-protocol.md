# Assessment 2 · Phenomenological Research Protocol

**Weighting:** 60% &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Word Limit:** 4,600 words (excluding references and interview extract)

---

## Task

Design a complete phenomenological research protocol for an original doctoral-level study in the domain of trauma, resilience, or adversity.

---

## Required Sections

### Section 1 — Title and Abstract (300 words)

Provide a full title that names the phenomenon, the population, and (optionally) the methodological approach. The abstract must specify: the phenomenon, the population, the level of eidetic generality sought, the method, and the anticipated contribution.

**Guidance:** The title and abstract should make it immediately clear this is a phenomenological study. The phenomenon should be precisely named — not 'resilience' but 'the experience of recovering a sense of future following prolonged bereavement.'

---

### Section 2 — Philosophical and Theoretical Framework (800 words)

Situate your study within the phenomenological tradition:
- Identify which thinkers are most relevant and why.
- Describe the key philosophical concepts your study draws on.
- Distinguish your approach from related traditions (IPA, grounded theory, narrative inquiry).
- Identify your level of eidetic generality and justify this choice.

---

### Section 3 — Methodology (1,000 words)

Describe and justify:
- **Method choice:** Giorgi, IPA, van Manen, Moustakas, or other — with justification.
- **Participant selection:** Inclusion/exclusion criteria; recruitment; sample size and sufficiency.
- **Data collection:** Interview design; opening question; at least two probe questions.
- **Analysis procedure:** Step-by-step, using your chosen method specifically.
- **Representation:** What will a reader see — individual descriptions, general structure, both?

---

### Section 4 — Ethical Framework (600 words)

Identify and address at least **four** ethical challenges specific to your study. At least two must be specifically phenomenological rather than generic qualitative research ethics concerns.

For each challenge: describe the issue → explain why it is significant for this study → describe your proposed response.

---

### Section 5 — Pilot Interview Extract and Analysis (1,500 words)

**Part A — Interview Extract** (min. 500 words, *not counted in word limit*)  
A verbatim extract from a pilot interview you have conducted, or a published phenomenological interview excerpt used with appropriate attribution.

**Part B — Preliminary Analysis** (1,000 words, *counted in word limit*)  
Demonstrate the first two steps of your chosen analysis procedure applied to the extract.

*If using Giorgi's method:* Reading for overall sense → delineation of meaning units (explain rationale for three) → preliminary transformation of three meaning units into psychological language.

*If using IPA:* Close reading and initial noting → emergent themes (list and explain three themes).

---

### Section 6 — Reflexive Memo (400 words)

A genuine reflexive memo documenting:
- Your prior assumptions about the phenomenon.
- The theoretical frameworks shaping how you think about it.
- Any personal experience (direct or professional) connecting you to the topic.
- Specific bracketing commitments: *"I will need to bracket my assumption that..."*

**Guidance:** Vague statements like 'I may have biases' are insufficient. Name specific assumptions and describe specific bracketing practices.

---

## Assessment Criteria

| Criterion | Description | Weighting |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Methodological rigour** | Method chosen, described, and justified with sophistication; analysis procedure applied correctly | 30% |
| **Philosophical grounding** | Philosophical choices principled and precise; correct use of primary phenomenological concepts | 25% |
| **Ethical sensitivity** | Genuinely phenomenological ethical challenges identified and addressed substantively | 15% |
| **Quality of pilot analysis** | Meaning units / initial noting applied correctly; phenomenological language used accurately | 15% |
| **Reflexive awareness** | Bracketing memo is specific, candid, and analytical | 10% |
| **Scholarly presentation** | APA 7th edition referencing; clear structure; fluent academic writing | 5% |

---

## Common Errors to Avoid

- **Describing the method without applying it.** Section 5 requires you to actually analyse the extract.
- **Generic ethics.** Maintaining confidentiality and obtaining informed consent are baseline requirements — not specifically phenomenological ethical challenges.
- **Imprecise phenomenon naming.** 'Resilience' is a concept, not a phenomenon. Name it at the level of lived experience.
- **Performative reflexivity.** Acknowledging you 'might have biases' without naming them is not a reflexive memo.
