# Phenomenology Teaching Materials

**Doctoral Seminar · A Phenomenological Approach to Trauma and Resilience**  
*Advanced PhD Programme · Department of Psychology*

---

## About This Resource

These materials support a doctoral seminar introducing PhD scholars to phenomenology as both a philosophical tradition and a rigorous empirical methodology for psychological research. The anchor text is *A Phenomenological Approach to Trauma and Resilience*.

The seminar spans **three contact sessions of ~3 hours each**, building progressively from philosophical foundations through psychological application to clinical and research implications.

---

## Seminar Structure

| Session | Title | Core Focus |
|---------|-------|------------|
| **Module 1** | Philosophical Foundations | Husserl, intentionality, epoché, eidetic analysis |
| **Module 2** | Psychological Phenomenology | Lifeworld, embodiment, temporality, intersubjectivity |
| **Module 3** | Trauma, Resilience & Applications | Trauma phenomena, Complex PTSD, resilience, clinical method |

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this seminar, doctoral scholars will be able to:

1. Articulate the philosophical foundations of phenomenology with doctoral-level precision
2. Distinguish phenomenological psychology from natural-scientific psychology and naive introspectionism
3. Apply eidetic analysis to psychological phenomena, including trauma and resilience
4. Demonstrate critical understanding of how phenomenological concepts illuminate traumatic experience
5. Evaluate the ethical and epistemological implications of studying vulnerable populations phenomenologically
6. Design the outline of an original phenomenological research project
