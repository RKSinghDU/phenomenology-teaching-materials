# Activity 02 · Epoché Walk

**Format:** Individual &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Topic:** Philosophical Foundations &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Time:** 15 minutes

---

## Description

Students walk silently for 10 minutes, actively trying to suspend their habitual labels and assumptions about what they see. They then write a brief description of what they noticed — particularly what surprised them when ordinary categories were suspended.

---

## Step-by-Step

1. Instructor introduces the epoché and the 'natural attitude' (5 min).
2. Students walk in silence, attempting to bracket prior knowledge — trying to see things as if for the first time, without immediately applying familiar names and categories.
3. On return, write for 5 minutes. The constraint: **no category labels** — no 'chair,' 'tree,' 'door.' Describe the thing as it appears without naming it.
4. Each student shares one sentence with the room.

---

## Materials

Paper or device for writing; access to a corridor, courtyard, or outdoor space.

---

## Debrief Guidance

Discuss how difficult it is to suspend even the most basic categories. This difficulty is precisely the point: the natural attitude is so deeply embedded we barely notice it is an *attitude* at all.

Ask: what did the bracketing reveal that ordinary seeing conceals? Students often notice texture, light, spatial relationships, and affective qualities that habitually recede behind the object-name.

Useful follow-up: if it is this hard to bracket the names of chairs and trees, how much harder is it to bracket prior theoretical and clinical assumptions when interviewing a trauma survivor? This connects the exercise directly to research ethics and methodology.
