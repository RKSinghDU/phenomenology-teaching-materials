## Suggested Sequencing

### Single seminar day (3 hours)
1. **Epoché walk** — opens the day with a felt experience of phenomenological attention
2. **The two cups** — introduces the distinction between objective and lived description
3. **Eidetic variation: anger vs. irritation** — applies free imaginative variation
4. **Lifeworld mapping** — bridges conceptual vocabulary to lived experience

### Three-session programme
- **Session 1:** Activities 01, 02, 03, 04 (foundations)
- **Session 2:** Activities 07, 08, 09, 10 (method)
- **Session 3:** Activities 05, 06, 11, 12 (trauma, ethics)

---

## A Note on Safety

The trauma-themed activities (05 and 06) deliberately avoid asking students to share personal traumatic material. They use published literary excerpts, fictional case scenarios, or non-traumatic lived experience. This is an intentional ethical modelling choice worth making explicit to students.
