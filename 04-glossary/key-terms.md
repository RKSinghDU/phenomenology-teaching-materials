# Glossary of Key Terms

Definitions for doctoral-level engagement — starting points for rigorous study rather than closed, final formulations.

---

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Assumptive World** | The set of deep, largely pre-reflective beliefs about the world's benevolence, meaningfulness, and one's own self-worth that constitute the background of ordinary experience. Trauma shatters these assumptions. |
| **Attunement (Stimmung)** | Heidegger's concept for the pre-reflective, bodily mood-orientation through which the world always already presents itself with a particular affective character. |
| **Complex PTSD (C-PTSD)** | An ICD-11 diagnostic category characterised by the classic PTSD symptom cluster plus affect dysregulation, altered self-perception, and relational disturbances; associated with prolonged, repeated interpersonal trauma. |
| **Dasein** | Heidegger's term for human existence — literally 'being-there' — emphasising that human being is always situated, temporal, and embedded in a world. |
| **Eidos / Essence** | The invariant, necessary structure of a type of experience — what makes instances of a kind the kind they are. The object of eidetic analysis. |
| **Eidetic Analysis** | The phenomenological procedure of free imaginative variation applied to examples of a type of experience to identify its essential (eidetic) structure. |
| **Embodiment (Leib / Körper)** | The distinction between the lived body as subject (Leib) and the objective body as object (Körper). |
| **Epoché** | The methodological suspension or bracketing of the natural attitude — temporarily setting aside presuppositions to attend to how the world presents itself to consciousness. |
| **Free Imaginative Variation** | The methodological procedure of mentally varying the features of an example to distinguish essential from accidental characteristics. |
| **Intentionality** | The structural property of consciousness whereby every act of consciousness is directed toward an object or content. Every experience is an experience *of* something. |
| **Intersubjectivity** | The fundamentally shared, social character of the lifeworld; the co-constitution of meaning between subjects; the basis of language, culture, and mutual understanding. |
| **Lifeworld (Lebenswelt)** | The pre-given, pre-theoretical, everyday world of lived experience — the taken-for-granted background of all human practice, perception, and meaning-making. |
| **Natural Attitude** | The ordinary, pre-reflective stance in which the world is assumed to be simply 'there,' independently of experience. The epoché brackets this attitude. |
| **Noema / Noesis** | The intentional object as experienced (noema) and the act of experiencing (noesis). Inseparable structural poles of intentional experience. |
| **Post-Traumatic Growth (PTG)** | Positive psychological transformation arising from the struggle with highly challenging circumstances — in personal strength, relationships, and existential meaning. |
| **Secondary Traumatic Stress** | Indirect traumatisation experienced by those who engage empathically with survivors' traumatic material; also called vicarious traumatisation or compassion fatigue. |
| **Somatic Flashback** | Traumatic re-experiencing encoded in bodily sensation, autonomic response, and postural organisation rather than in declarative narrative memory. |
| **Spatiality (Lived Space)** | The felt, meaningful, bodily-social character of space as experienced — home, safety, threat, intimacy — as opposed to geometric, measurable space. |
| **Temporality (Lived Time)** | The flowing, layered structure of human time-consciousness — past retentions, present engagements, and future anticipations — as opposed to clock time. |
| **Thrownness (Geworfenheit)** | Heidegger's term for the condition of human existence as always already 'thrown' into a world, body, culture, and historical situation not of one's choosing. |

## Key Distinctions

### Phenomenology vs. Introspectionism

| | Phenomenology | Introspectionism |
|---|---|---|
| **Method** | Eidetic analysis of lived structure | Trained observation to isolate sensory elements |
| **Focus** | Holistic, intentional, meaningful experience | Elementary sensations and their combinations |
| **Goal** | Essential structures of experience types | Laws of mental composition |

### Lived Body (Leib) vs. Objective Body (Körper)

| | Leib | Körper |
|---|---|---|
| **Perspective** | First-person, from within | Third-person, from outside |
| **Character** | Transparent, pre-reflective, skilled | Opaque, objectified, measurable |
| **In trauma** | Becomes hyper-present, obtrusive, alien | Site of symptom and dysfunction |

### Shame vs. Guilt

| | Shame | Guilt |
|---|---|---|
| **Object** | The self — "I am bad" | The action — "I did something bad" |
| **Requires witness?** | Yes — real or imagined | No |
| **Motivational pull** | Hiding, withdrawal, or rage | Reparation, apology, behavioural change |
